# Right-Wing Intelligence Brief
**Cycle: 18-19 April 2026**

---

The dominant story of the past 48 hours is the Milan rally of the **Patriots for Europe** grouping -- a coordinated effort to project far-right unity in Europe following the crushing defeat of **Viktor Orbán** in Hungary -- which was undercut by modest turnout, Orbán's conspicuous absence, and widening internal contradictions. Simultaneously, the **Rassemblement National** made aggressive moves to court French business elites ahead of 2027, while the MAGA world processed a bruising week for **JD Vance** and escalating institutional conflicts over elections, religion, and the Iran war. In the UK, **Reform UK** faces a compound credibility crisis just weeks before crucial local elections, as tax avoidance allegations against its deputy leader pile up.

---

## The Milan Rally: Far-Right Solidarity Performance Meets Post-Orbán Reality

The centerpiece event of the cycle was Saturday's Patriots for Europe rally in Milan's Piazza del Duomo, billed as "Without Fear -- In Europe, Masters in Our Own Home!" and organized by **Matteo Salvini** of Italy's **Lega**. The gathering brought together **Jordan Bardella** (**Rassemblement National**), **Geert Wilders** (**PVV**), and Czech PM **Andrej Babiš**, among others [[50]](https://www.rfi.fr/en/international/20260419-europe-s-far-right-seeks-to-regroup-at-anti-immigration-rally-in-milan).

The optics were double-edged. The rally was explicitly framed as a show of resilience after Orbán's landslide defeat the previous weekend [[54]](https://kuwaittimes.com/article/42489/world/europe-far-right-gathers-in-milan-after-orban-defeat/amp), but the Hungarian co-founder of the Patriots was conspicuously absent [[62]](https://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/politica/2026/04/18/a-milano-corteo-dei-patrioti-e-della-lega-in-piazza-duomo.-salvini-chiude_c310da29-ad7e-4873-af8f-09317aa97b3f.html), and Fidesz sent no meaningful delegation. The crowd figures told their own story: Italian left-wing media estimated around 2,000 marchers and perhaps double that in the square itself, with Salvini urging a sparse crowd to "make yourselves heard" [[66]](https://ilmanifesto.it/milano-il-popolo-leghista-non-si-vede-in-pochi-seguono-salvini). Counter-demonstrations drew an estimated 30,000 across multiple Milan piazzas, including an unusual anti-remigration event organized by coalition partner **Forza Italia** [[69]](https://www.radiopopolare.it/la-milano-antifascista-respinge-i-leghisti-di-salvini) -- a significant intra-right rupture that signals Berlusconi's heirs are repositioning toward the centre as the sovereignist wave shows signs of cresting.

The rally's rhetorical content was revealing. The word "remigration" -- which had dominated promotion of the event -- was notably soft-pedalled by Salvini himself on stage, who offered a "points-based residence permit" framing rather than explicit mass deportation [[60]](https://tg24.sky.it/politica/2026/04/18/manifestazione-lega-milano-salvini), reflecting pressure from both coalition partners and from the diplomatic fallout of Trump's break with Pope Leo XIV and **Giorgia Meloni** [[63]](https://ilmanifesto.it/salvini-sfida-il-25-aprile-milano-prepara-la-risposta). Wilders was far less restrained, warning of a "tsunami of mass immigration, mainly from Islamic countries" [[103]](https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20260418-europe-s-far-right-leaders-gather-in-milan-rally-against-immigration). Bardella positioned himself between these poles: more measured in tone but unambiguous on victory: "Our victory in the upcoming presidential election is within reach" [[37]](https://www.franceinfo.fr/politique/jordan-bardella/reportage-en-italie-jordan-bardella-estime-qu-une-victoire-du-rn-en-2027-serait-une-victoire-pour-toutes-les-nations-d-europe_7947992.html).

A critical subtext is the fracturing of the transatlantic nationalist alliance. Multiple reports note that European far-right parties -- including the **AfD** -- are publicly distancing themselves from Trump, whose attacks on the Pope and on Meloni have embarrassed their domestic positioning [[121]](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-war-iran-pope-afd-orban-meloni-le-pen). Salvini's call to end EU sanctions on Russian gas and oil imports [[60]](https://tg24.sky.it/politica/2026/04/18/manifestazione-lega-milano-salvini) underlines that the rally's anti-Brussels thrust is in some tension with NATO solidarity, a growing fault line within European right-nationalism.

## RN and the French 2027 Escalation: Business Courtship and Internal Alignment

The French far right is operating in full presidential-cycle mode. Bardella's Milan speech was explicitly framed around 2027, and his travel to Italy coincided with a landmark domestic move: an imminent lunch with the **Medef** (France's main employers' federation), described by France Inter as an unprecedented "anointing" of the RN by big business [[47]](https://www.humanite.fr/politique/entreprise/extreme-droite-le-grand-patronat-adoube-le-rn-pour-2027). Marine Le Pen had already dined with the likes of Bernard Arnault and the CEO of TotalEnergies [[56]](https://www.nouvelobs.com/opinions/20260418.OBS114268/marine-le-pen-face-aux-grands-patrons-comme-un-mauvais-remake-de-l-ordre-du-jour.html). Bardella framed the outreach as structural: "To win in 2027, you need the popular bloc and the entrepreneurial elite behind you" [[40]](https://www.bfmtv.com/politique/front-national/jordan-bardella-va-ecrire-aux-chefs-d-entreprise-pour-solliciter-des-entretiens-et-lister-les-normes-et-impots-qui-pesent-sur-la-production-de-richesse_AN-202604180259.html).

Internal party dynamics are more complex. Reports of a tension-filled presidential seminar held on 16-17 April -- in a "secret location" -- note that the stated goal was "alignment" between the Bardella and Le Pen camps ahead of the vote, with one RN parliamentarian insisting "there was perfect agreement, no notable opposition or tension" [[49]](https://www.lepoint.fr/politique/rn-dans-les-coulisses-du-seminaire-presidentiel-secret-de-marine-le-pen-et-jordan-bardella-W6SA43I6EVBYLFDTDXT5ARGNQM/). Yet gossip magazine and opposition media coverage points to strategic divergences, particularly over EU positioning and the removal of European flags from RN-run town halls [[44]](https://www.gala.fr/l_actu/news_de_stars/du-rififi-entre-jordan-bardella-et-marine-le-pen-a-un-an-de-la-presidentielle-la-tension-monte-il-y-a-deux-camps-20260417). The simultaneous news cycle around Bardella's relationship with an Italian aristocrat [[45]](https://www.gala.fr/l_actu/news_de_stars/jordan-bardella-en-couple-avec-maria-carolina-de-bourbon-des-deux-siciles-deviendra-t-il-prince-s-ils-se-marient-20260416) is being managed carefully -- some within the RN see image benefit, others warn it undermines his *banlieue* outsider narrative [[45]](https://www.gala.fr/l_actu/news_de_stars/jordan-bardella-en-couple-avec-maria-carolina-de-bourbon-des-deux-siciles-deviendra-t-il-prince-s-ils-se-marient-20260416).

A notable geopolitical wrinkle: Bardella explicitly called Trump's Middle East policy "totally erratic," noting that "the truths of Monday are not necessarily the truths of Tuesday" [[55]](https://www.middleeastmonitor.com/20260418-frances-bardella-calls-trumps-middle-east-policy-erratic/) -- a rare and pointed public distancing from the American right that mirrors Salvini's own discomfort with Trump's papal conflict.

## MAGA and the Iran War: Vance's Diminishment and the Pope Conflict

The past week has been structurally damaging for **JD Vance**. After flying to Hungary to campaign for Orbán (who then lost) [[6]](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/27/j-d-vances-bumpy-ride), being dispatched to negotiate with Iran (talks collapsed after 21 hours) [[6]](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/27/j-d-vances-bumpy-ride), and becoming the administration's frontman in the theological dispute with **Pope Leo XIV**, Vance enters the weekend as the emblem of MAGA's overextension. As one analyst put it, Vance is "no longer Trump's obvious successor" and has "no such thing as a Vance base" within the modern GOP [[16]](https://theweek.com/politics/jd-vance-iran-pope-maga-veep).

The Pope Leo XIV conflict is acquiring structural significance beyond a news cycle. CNN frames it as a clash between two competing Christianities: the Pope's historical Jesus versus the "warrior Christ" of MAGA, invoking Pete Hegseth reading a fake Bible quote from Pulp Fiction at a Pentagon prayer service [[8]](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/19/politics/pope-leo-trump-christianity-maga-jesus). This is not merely a culture-war skirmish -- it risks alienating the Catholic and mainline Protestant vote at a moment when Trump's Iran war is producing domestic economic pain.

Trump's Turning Point USA rally in Phoenix ("Build the Red Wall") [[153]](https://www.kjzz.org/photos-president-donald-trump-attends-phoenix-turning-point-usa-rally) was explicitly midterm-oriented, but reporting noted thinner crowds than expected, particularly among younger voters -- consistent with polling showing 70% disapproval among under-30s [[149]](https://www.salon.com/2026/04/18/from-campuses-to-classrooms-turning-point-usas-next-frontier/). The administration's simultaneous move to block appointments to the federal voting machine oversight panel [[13]](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/trump-adminstration-block-appointments-eac-voting-equipment-standards-committee/), and the ongoing White House ballroom legal saga [[3]](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/apr/18/trump-administration-white-house-ballroom), reflect a broader pattern of institutional consolidation that is proceeding even as the political coalition shows stress fractures.

## Reform UK: The Tax Scandal and Pre-Election Vulnerabilities

**Reform UK** enters the 7 May English local elections under a compound integrity assault. Deputy leader **Richard Tice** faces detailed reporting from the Sunday Times and Tax Policy Associates showing he ran four shell companies that did not pay approximately £98,000 in corporation tax between 2020 and 2022, with those savings flowing through to a parent company that donated over £1.1 million to Reform [[76]](https://www.thetimes.com/uk/politics/article/richard-tice-corporation-tax-reform-ttq9cp60f). A second layer of the same investigation found failures in Tice's REIT-related tax obligations amounting to a further ~£120,000 [[75]](https://taxpolicy.org.uk/2026/04/18/richard-tice-quidnet-reit-corporation-tax/). The total exposure across onshore and offshore entities now stands at nearly £800,000 [[76]](https://www.thetimes.com/uk/politics/article/richard-tice-corporation-tax-reform-ttq9cp60f).

Tice's response -- "a long career with multiple businesses is bound to feature some errors" [[84]](https://www.v2radio.co.uk/news/v2-radio-politics-news/reforms-richard-tice-responds-to-reported-failure-to-pay-and163100000-tax/) -- sits awkwardly against his prior public attacks on Angela Rayner over stamp duty underpayments [[85]](https://www.independent.co.uk/bulletin/news/reform-richard-tice-tax-hmrc-b2960553.html). The party's vetting crisis continues in parallel: two more local election candidates have been found to have made racist and Islamophobic social media posts [[79]](https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2026/apr/18/two-more-reform-local-election-candidates-accused-offensive-posts), and Reform's Worcestershire council group has deposed its own leader just under a year after taking power [[92]](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c1w4yqxqzezo).

The party's trajectory nonetheless remains strong at the macro level. **Nigel Farage** is projecting confidence ahead of May 7, claiming to be "tapping into patriotic old Labour" and predicting "an electoral earthquake" [[91]](https://www.thetimes.com/uk/politics/article/nigel-farage-reform-uk-interview-latest-jww3tvnsz). The tensions between organisational chaos and polling momentum -- and the question of whether the tax scandal is damaging enough to dent that momentum -- make Reform the most electorally consequential story in British politics right now.

## Hungary: Orbán's Collapse and Tisza's Historic Supermajority

The final vote count confirmed **Tisza Párt** leader **Péter Magyar** won 141 of 199 parliamentary seats -- 53.18% of the party list vote versus Fidesz's 38.61% [[191]](https://telex.hu/belfold/2026/04/18/valasztas-vegeredmeny-mandatumok-tisza-fidesz-mi-hazank). This is the largest parliamentary majority since Hungary's post-communist transition, and surpasses Fidesz's own best result of 135 seats [[191]](https://telex.hu/belfold/2026/04/18/valasztas-vegeredmeny-mandatumok-tisza-fidesz-mi-hazank). Orbán, in a rare interview, acknowledged "a political era has ended" and called for "complete renewal" within Fidesz [[196]](https://www.latimes.com/world-nation/story/2026-04-17/hungarys-orban-says-complete-renewal-needed-within-his-party-after-election-loss), though he did not step down as party leader.

Magyar moved immediately: he is seeking to bring forward parliament's formation to the week of 4 May, has announced his parliamentary group leader [[188]](https://www.forbes.hu/hirek/tisza-frakcio-bujdoso-andrea/), held informal talks with the European Commission on frozen EU funds [[189]](https://www.portfolio.hu/gazdasag/20260419/valasztas-2026-soha-nem-latott-tobbseg-a-tisza-part-szamara-folyamatban-a-kormanyalakitas-831452), and received congratulations from UK PM Keir Starmer, who discussed Ukraine solidarity [[192]](https://www.portfolio.hu/gazdasag/20260418/valasztas-2026-mar-141-mandatumon-all-a-tisza-a-fidesz-kepviselok-szama-52-re-csokkent-831380). Heritage Foundation commentary frames Orbán's migration policies as having won the "argument" even as he lost the election -- pointing to the EU Parliament's recent vote on offshore asylum processing [[175]](https://www.heritage.org/europe/commentary/viktor-orban-lost-the-election-he-won-the-argument) -- a framing that serves the broader far-right narrative that their ideas have mainstreamed even when their leaders lose.

The Milan rally's rhetorical solidarity with Orbán thus reads as partly a face-saving exercise for a grouping that has lost its most totemic European figure.

## AfD: Record Polling Amid Local Electoral Tests

**Alternative für Deutschland** has reached its highest-ever polling lead over Chancellor **Friedrich Merz's** CDU/CSU in Germany, hitting 27% to the Union's 24% in the INSA Sunday trend -- a 3-point gap described as "the largest ever" in that survey [[205]](https://www.tagesspiegel.de/politik/cducsu-nur-noch-bei-24-prozent-afd-liegt-in-neuer-umfrage-drei-punkte-vor-der-union-15496539.html). Multiple polling firms now show the AfD in first place nationally [[208]](https://www.welt.de/politik/deutschland/article69e37fcf769e68aeef7a6b28/insa-sonntagstrend-afd-liegt-in-umfrage-drei-prozentpunkte-vor-union.html).

A local-level test was underway simultaneously in Brandenburg's Uckermark district, where AfD candidate **Felix Teichner** was bidding to become the party's first *Landrat* (county executive) in Brandenburg -- a symbolically significant breakthrough for a party still classified as "confirmed right-extremist" by Brandenburg's constitutional protection office [[204]](https://www.tagesspiegel.de/berlin/landratswahlen-die-uckermark-wahlt-afd-will-erstmals-landrat-stellen-15497023.html). A campaign controversy erupted when singer Heino -- long associated in some minds with German folk nationalism -- sued Teichner for €250,000 in damages after the candidate falsely implied Heino endorsed him [[197]](https://www.welt.de/politik/deutschland/article69e484da064e04c985e98b7e/wahlkampf-in-brandenburg-heino-verklagt-afd-politiker-und-fordert-250-000-euro-schmerzensgeld.html). Heino's manager was explicit: "He has publicly spoken out against the AfD."

A separate Welt analysis notes that while the national "firewall" (Brandmauer) against AfD business engagement remains nominally intact, regional business communities in AfD strongholds are quietly normalising contact with the party [[201]](https://www.zeit.de/wirtschaft/2026-04/alternative-fuer-deutschland-wirtschaft-zusammenarbeit-uckermark-landrat).

## Prawo i Sprawiedliwość: Internal Fracture Accelerating

Poland's main opposition party **PiS** is in acute internal crisis. Former PM **Mateusz Morawiecki** has established a new association, *Rozwój Plus*, drawing 38 PiS politicians -- prompting **Jarosław Kaczyński** to issue list-placement ultimatums [[210]](https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/morawiecki-nie-chce-wystapic-z-czarnkiem-posel-pis-w-ogniu-pytan/hwtmywn). Morawiecki insists the association is "expert, not political" and compatible with PiS ideology [[211]](https://www.rmf24.pl/polityka/news-politycy-pis-wymieniaja-ciosy-roznie-mamy-naostrzone-kredki-,nId,8078045), but PiS figures from the **Solidarna Polska** wing, led by MEP **Patryk Jaki**, accuse him of undermining party discipline and benefiting the ruling coalition of PM Donald Tusk [[214]](https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/patryk-jaki-oskarza-ludzi-mateusza-morawieckiego-zamiescil-ostry-wpis/c5b93ed). Social media exchanges Saturday night descended into open mudslinging [[212]](https://www.rp.pl/polityka/art44184301-stowarzyszenie-morawieckiego-dzieli-pis-starcie-poslow-w-mediach-spolecznosciowych), with factions publicly disputing whether Morawiecki is refusing to campaign alongside presidential candidate **Przemysław Czarnek** [[210]](https://wiadomosci.onet.pl/kraj/morawiecki-nie-chce-wystapic-z-czarnkiem-posel-pis-w-ogniu-pytan/hwtmywn). A Monday meeting between Morawiecki and Kaczyński is anticipated; the outcome will determine whether this is a managed rebellion or the first serious PiS split in 15 years.

## Watchlist: Three Stories to Track

1. **AfD's Uckermark Landrat bid (result pending):** If Teichner wins, the AfD secures its first county executive in Brandenburg, a threshold moment in the party's normalisation at local governance level -- watch for reactions from the CDU and the Brandmauer debate.

2. **RN's Medef lunch and the 2027 business-elite alignment:** Bardella's meeting with French employers this week is a genuine structural test of whether the RN's economic repositioning can lock in elite support before a possible Le Pen presidential run -- or whether it generates a backlash within the party's working-class base.

3. **PiS split dynamics and the Morawiecki-Kaczyński meeting:** The Monday meeting could either defuse or formalise a factional split that would reshape the Polish opposition ahead of presidential and parliamentary cycles. A formal rupture would realign Polish right-wing politics significantly and potentially benefit Tusk's coalition.
