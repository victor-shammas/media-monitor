## European Far-Right: Unity, Disunity, and Electoral Shifts

A significant development saw **Hungary's** nationalist **Fidesz** party, led by **Viktor Orbán**, suffer a "dramatic landslide defeat" after 16 years in power, losing to the opposition **Tisza** party [[195]](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g40npz37lo) [[196]](https://www.latimes.com/world-nation/story/2026-04-17/hungarys-orban-says-complete-renewal-needed-within-his-party-after-election-loss). The **Tisza** party secured an "absolute record" super-majority of 141 out of 199 seats [[191]](https://telex.hu/belfold/2026/04/18/valasztas-vegeredmeny-mandatumok-tisza-fidesz-mi-hazank) [[195]](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g40npz37lo). **Orbán** conceded, acknowledging that "a political era has ended" and calling for "complete renewal" within **Fidesz**, though he did not indicate he would step down as party president [[196]](https://www.latimes.com/world-nation/story/2026-04-17/hungarys-orban-says-complete-renewal-needed-within-his-party-after-election-loss). This outcome is widely seen as a setback for Europe's far-right parties that had viewed **Orbán** as a model [[97]](https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/highlight/2026/04/19/leftist-leaders-gather-in-spain-to-rally-against-rise-of-far-right) [[103]](https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20260418-europe-s-far-right-leaders-gather-in-milan-rally-against-immigration). However, some analyses, including from **The Heritage Foundation**, suggested that while **Orbán** lost the election, his hardline migration policies have become an "emerging consensus" across Europe, influencing recent EU measures [[175]](https://www.heritage.org/europe/commentary/viktor-orban-lost-the-election-he-won-the-argument).
