**Executive Summary**

The European far-right demonstrated a unified front against immigration and EU bureaucracy at a major rally in Milan, even as the significant electoral defeat of **Viktor Orbán** in Hungary sent ripples through the movement. Concurrently, **Jordan Bardella** of France's **Rassemblement National** intensified efforts to mainstream his party by engaging with business elites and moderating certain historical stances. In the US, **J.D. Vance**'s difficult week underscored the absolute subordination required by **Donald Trump**, whose foreign policy is framed as a return to overt American imperialism and whose domestic narrative champions a strong "law and order" agenda.

## Transnational Far-Right Mobilization and EU Dynamics

A significant convergence of European far-right leaders took place in Milan, hosted by **Matteo Salvini**'s **Lega**, bringing together figures such as **Jordan Bardella** (**Rassemblement National**, France) and **Geert Wilders** (PVV, Netherlands) [[26]](https://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/politica/2026/04/18/a-milano-corteo-dei-patrioti-e-della-lega-in-piazza-duomo.-salvini-chiude_c310da29-ad7e-4873-af8f-09317aa97b3f.html) [[47]](https://www.thelocal.it/20260418/europes-far-right-gathers-in-milan-after-orban-defeat) [[48]](https://www.rte.ie/news/world/2026/0418/1568882-europe-politics/) [[52]](https://www.straitstimes.com/world/europe/europes-far-right-gathers-in-milan-after-orban-defeat). The rally, under the slogan 'In Europa padroni a casa nostra' ('In Europe, masters in our own home'), focused heavily on anti-immigration sentiment, with **Salvini** explicitly using the term "remigration" for repatriations [[26]](https://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/politica/2026/04/18/a-milano-corteo-dei-patrioti-e-della-lega-in-piazza-duomo.-salvini-chiude_c310da29-ad7e-4873-af8f-09317aa97b3f.html). **Wilders** articulated fears of a "tsunami of mass immigration, illegal immigration, mostly from Islamic countries," targeting "the original inhabitants of Europe" [[48]](https://www.rte.ie/news/world/2026/0418/1568882-europe-politics/). Beyond immigration, the event also attacked the European Union and International Monetary Fund, advocating for an end to the Green Deal and stability pact [[26]](https://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/politica/2026/04/18/a-milano-corteo-dei-patrioti-e-della-lega-in-piazza-duomo.-salvini-chiude_c310da29-ad7e-4873-af8f-09317aa97b3f.html).

This far-right gathering occurred in direct opposition to a simultaneous summit of leftist leaders in Barcelona, highlighting the deepening political polarization on the continent [[38]](https://uk.news.yahoo.com/leftist-leaders-gather-spain-rally-052007857.html) [[39]](https://www.devdiscourse.com/article/politics/3878716-defending-democracy-leaders-unite-in-barcelona-against-far-right-extremism) [[40]](https://kuwaittimes.com/article/42471/world/leftist-leaders-gather-in-spain-to-rally-against-rise-of-far-right/) [[43]](https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20260418-sanchez-lula-rally-left-wing-leaders-in-spain-amid-far-right-surge) [[45]](https://www.thelocal.es/20260418/leftist-leaders-gather-in-spain-to-rally-against-rise-of-far-right) [[46]](https://www.telesurenglish.net/global-south-leaders-fascism-spain/) [[55]](https://en.mercopress.com/2026/04/17/lula-and-sanchez-seal-progressive-alliance-in-barcelona-and-call-for-front-against-global-far-right) [[56]](https://www.msn.com/en-gb/news/other/ar-AA2168tm). A notable absence from the Milan rally was **Viktor Orbán**, who was thanked and supported by **Salvini** despite his recent electoral defeat in Hungary [[26]](https://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/politica/2026/04/18/a-milano-corteo-dei-patrioti-e-della-lega-in-piazza-duomo.-salvini-chiude_c310da29-ad7e-4873-af8f-09317aa97b3f.html) [[47]](https://www.thelocal.it/20260418/europes-far-right-gathers-in-milan-after-orban-defeat) [[48]](https://www.rte.ie/news/world/2026/0418/1568882-europe-politics/). This defeat, which ended **Orbán**'s 16 years in power, is widely acknowledged as a setback for Europe's far-right, although some sources note that many of his once-derided migration policies are now being adopted by the EU itself [[38]](https://uk.news.yahoo.com/leftist-leaders-gather-spain-rally-052007857.html) [[40]](https://kuwaittimes.com/article/42471/world/leftist-leaders-gather-in-spain-to-rally-against-rise-of-far-right/) [[45]](https://www.thelocal.es/20260418/leftist-leaders-gather-in-spain-to-rally-against-rise-of-far-right) [[47]](https://www.thelocal.it/20260418/europes-far-right-gathers-in-milan-after-orban-defeat) [[48]](https://www.rte.ie/news/world/2026/0418/1568882-europe-politics/) [[52]](https://www.straitstimes.com/world/europe/europes-far-right-gathers-in-milan-after-orban-defeat) [[61]](https://www.heritage.org/europe/commentary/viktor-orban-lost-the-election-he-won-the-argument). **Orbán** himself conceded a need for "complete renewal" within his **Fidesz** party, describing the loss as "painful" and marking the "end of an era" [[65]](https://www.latimes.com/world-nation/story/2026-04-17/hungarys-orban-says-complete-renewal-needed-within-his-party-after-election-loss). His party lost several long-held strongholds to the opposition **Tisza** party [[63]](https://www.hungarianconservative.com/articles/politics/tisza-party-peter-magyar-fidesz-orban-hungary-election-final-vote-count/). The **Sverigedemokraterna**'s support for **Orbán** is critically framed as a "historical betrayal" [[10]](https://www.dn.se/ledare/peter-wolodarski-sds-stod-for-orban-ar-inte-ett-misstag-det-ar-ett-historiskt-svek/).

## Right-Wing Domestic Politics and Leadership

In the United States, **Donald Trump**'s continued dominance within the MAGA movement was evident, with **Vice-President J.D. Vance** experiencing a "bumpy ride" and a "very bad week" that underscored his subordinate position [[3]](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/18/opinion/hungary-election-orban-loses-trump-maga.html) [[4]](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/27/j-d-vances-bumpy-ride). **Vance**'s diplomatic efforts, including attending a rally for **Viktor Orbán** in Hungary and being dispatched to Pakistan for Iran talks, were framed as errands from **Trump**, with his dignity contingent on his obedience [[4]](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/27/j-d-vances-bumpy-ride). The ongoing "grudge match" between **Trump** and **Pope Leo XIV** was also highlighted as a source of contention [[2]](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2026/apr/18/trump-pope-war-morals) [[4]](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/27/j-d-vances-bumpy-ride). On foreign policy, **Trump**'s approach is characterized as a return to "American imperialism," with threats against Iran, Venezuela, Cuba, and Greenland, focused on "plunder[ing] the planet of its land, labor, and vital resources" [[5]](https://inkstickmedia.com/how-donald-trump-learned-to-love-american-imperialism/). Domestically, **The Heritage Foundation** contrasted what it described as rising carjackings under the **Biden** administration with a hypothetical "second Trump administration" scenario in 2025, which would see a sharp decline in crime and a dramatic improvement in law enforcement response, including calls for prosecuting juveniles as adults [[60]](https://www.heritage.org/crime-and-justice/commentary/the-dc-carjacking-crisis-tale-two-administrations).

In France, **Jordan Bardella**'s **Rassemblement National** is actively pursuing a strategy to broaden its appeal, seeking to bridge the "bloc populaire" and the "élite entrepreneuriale" [[13]](https://www.bfmtv.com/politique/front-national/jordan-bardella-va-ecrire-aux-chefs-d-entreprise-pour-solliciter-des-entretiens-et-lister-les-normes-et-impots-qui-pesent-sur-la-production-de-richesse_AN-202604180259.html). **Bardella** announced plans to solicit feedback from business leaders on "norms and taxes that weigh on the production of wealth," and subsequently dined with the bureau exécutif of the Medef, marking a significant step towards mainstream acceptance [[13]](https://www.bfmtv.com/politique/front-national/jordan-bardella-va-ecrire-aux-chefs-d-entreprise-pour-solliciter-des-entretiens-et-lister-les-normes-et-impots-qui-pesent-sur-la-production-de-richesse_AN-202604180259.html) [[21]](https://www.humanite.fr/politique/entreprise/extreme-droite-le-grand-patronat-adoube-le-rn-pour-2027). This follows a similar dinner by **Marine Le Pen** with top French capitalists, reflecting a deliberate "dédiabolisation" strategy [[14]](https://www.huffingtonpost.fr/politique/article/louis-aliot-profite-d-un-deal-avec-la-droite-pour-offrir-au-rassemblement-national-un-succes-majeur_262806.html) [[23]](https://www.nouvelobs.com/opinions/20260418.OBS114268/marine-le-pen-face-aux-grands-patrons-comme-un-mauvais-remake-de-l-ordre-du-jour.html). Internally, however, there are reports of "dissensions of size" between **Bardella** and **Le Pen** over strategic shifts, particularly on moderating stances on the European Union [[18]](https://www.gala.fr/l_actu/news_de_stars/du-rififi-entre-jordan-bardella-et-marine-le-pen-a-un-an-de-la-presidentielle-la-tension-monte-il-y-a-deux-camps-20260417).

Norway's **Fremskrittspartiet** (**Frp**) continued to focus on its core anti-state, pro-market ideology, with a debate piece asserting "The state is our servant - not our master" and criticizing the **Labour Party**'s economic policies for high prices and inflation [[8]](https://www.nettavisen.no/norsk-debatt/staten-er-var-tjener-ikke-var-herre/o/5-95-2988973). The party also showed local-level pragmatic engagement, discussing budget cooperation with **Høyre** [[6]](https://www.sandnesposten.no/onsker-ikke-at-kine-overgangen-skal-komme-mellom-frp-og-hoyre/s/5-105-1349202) and even an unusual, quickly disavowed, joint invitation with the far-left **Rødt** for a trade union seminar [[7]](https://www.aftenposten.no/norge/politikk/i/aJO922/marie-sneve-martinussen-sendte-ut-fellesinvitasjon-fra-roedt-og-frp-da-reagerte-frp-toppen).

## Immigration, Identity, and Cultural Wars

Anti-immigration rhetoric remains a potent force across the right and far-right. In the UK, **Reform UK** continues to push boundaries, with **Nigel Farage** suggesting outer London boroughs like Bromley and Havering should be offered a vote to rejoin their "parent counties," implicitly linking this to these areas retaining "demographics comparable to the country at large" as London becomes an "internationalised city-state" [[32]](https://unherd.com/newsroom/nigel-farage-shouldnt-let-bromley-leave-london/) [[33]](https://unherd.com/newsroom/nigel-farage-shouldnt-let-bromley-leave-london/?edition=us). The party also faced controversy as two local election candidates were accused of offensive social media posts, including racist and anti-Islam comments, with one calling Islam "the religion of rape, incest and paedophilia" [[30]](https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2026/apr/18/two-more-reform-local-election-candidates-accused-offensive-posts). Public support for far-right activist **Tommy Robinson** also featured, with **Sharon Osbourne**'s public endorsement of an upcoming "Unite the Kingdom" rally leading to a homelessness charity cutting ties with her [[36]](https://www.thenational.scot/news/26033097.charity-cuts-ties-sharon-osbourne-far-right-support/) [[42]](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/home-news/sharon-osbourne-tommy-robinson-rally-centrepoint-b2960135.html) [[44]](https://www.nme.com/news/music/homelessness-charity-cuts-ties-with-sharon-osbourne-after-she-appears-to-lend-support-to-tommy-robinson-far-right-rally-this-sort-of-event-does-not-align-with-our-values-3941007).

The broader cultural war against feminism and the rise of the "manosphere" also drew attention. An article noted a growing societal unease with "relationships between the sexes," declining birth rates, and rising loneliness, with some political figures offering solutions seen as a "nostalgic fanciful world" promoting traditional gender roles [[35]](https://johnmenadue.com/post/2026/04/from-feminism-to-the-manosphere-where-to-now/). The influence of the "manosphere" is prompting concern, with a former RAF pilot describing subtle but alarming shifts in her sons' attitudes and language [[49]](https://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/entry/subtle-signs-manosphere-impact-sons_uk_69e0c1d7e4b023b635da4936). This movement is increasingly associated with "anti-women and anti-feminist sentiments" [[49]](https://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/entry/subtle-signs-manosphere-impact-sons_uk_69e0c1d7e4b023b635da4936). Interestingly, some "manosphere bros" are reportedly "sick of Trump as MAGA fractures," indicating internal friction within the broader right-wing ecosystem [[54]](http://www.msn.com/en-us/news/politics/what-kind-of-delusional-reality-are-we-living-in-manosphere-bros-sick-of-trump-as-maga-fractures/vi-AA219REU). The radicalization of individuals, particularly older relatives, through online exposure to conspiracy theories and far-right agitators like **Tommy Robinson** was also highlighted, with one mother believing **Keir Starmer** aims to "replace us all with Muslims" [[50]](https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2026/apr/18/the-families-torn-apart-by-older-relatives-going-far-right).
