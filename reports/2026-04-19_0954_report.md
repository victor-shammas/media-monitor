**Executive Summary:**

The European far-right held a significant rally in Milan, championing "remigration" and anti-EU policies, yet its momentum was notably tempered by the recent electoral defeat of Hungarian nationalist **Viktor Orbán**. Concurrently, prominent right-wing figures in the US and UK faced domestic scrutiny, from tax controversies and internal party disagreements to legal challenges. A global progressive counter-summit in Barcelona framed Orbán's loss as a pivotal moment to collectively push back against rising far-right extremism, highlighting the increasing international polarization.

## Domestic Right-Wing Political Maneuvering

In the United States, former President **Donald Trump** continued to headline events, including a "Build the Red Wall" rally in Phoenix hosted by **Turning Point USA** [[75]](https://www.djournal.com/news/nation-world/photos-show-the-build-the-red-wall-rally-in-arizona-headlined-by-trump/article_69bbaea4-c1fc-5b23-b18e-c783db48fc36.html) [[78]](https://nevalleynews.org/20779/news/protesters-line-the-streets-as-maga-supporters-trickle-in-to-the-turning-point-usa-event-with-trump-in-phoenix/) [[81]](https://www.kjzz.org/photos-president-donald-trump-attends-phoenix-turning-point-usa-rally). Despite **Turning Point USA's** stated goals of expanding influence in high schools, reports indicated that **Trump's** rally primarily attracted an older demographic, and his disapproval ratings among younger voters remain high [[77]](https://www.salon.com/2026/04/18/from-campuses-to-classrooms-turning-point-usas-next-frontier/). Meanwhile, **Vice President J.D. Vance** experienced a challenging period, described as a "very bad week" and a "bumpy ride," particularly after his visit to Hungary to support **Viktor Orbán** was quickly followed by **Orbán's** electoral defeat [[5]](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/18/opinion/hungary-election-orban-loses-trump-maga.html) [[6]](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/27/j-d-vances-bumpy-ride). **Vance** was reportedly "subordinated" to **Trump**, being dispatched to Pakistan for negotiations and facing criticism amid **Trump's** attack on **Pope Leo XIV** [[6]](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/27/j-d-vances-bumpy-ride).

In France, **Rassemblement National** (**RN**) President **Jordan Bardella** is actively engaged in efforts to broaden the party's appeal, reaching out to business elites to discuss economic reforms and deregulation [[13]](https://www.lejdd.fr/politique/rn-marine-le-pen-a-la-table-des-grands-patrons-171825) [[17]](https://www.bfmtv.com/politique/front-national/jordan-bardella-va-ecrire-aux-chefs-d-entreprise-pour-solliciter-des-entretiens-et-lister-les-normes-et-impots-qui-pesent-sur-la-production-de-richesse_AN-202604180259.html) [[25]](https://www.humanite.fr/politique/entreprise/extreme-droite-le-grand-patronat-adoube-le-rn-pour-2027). This strategic outreach reportedly causes internal "dissensions" with **Marine Le Pen**, particularly regarding moderating positions on displaying the **European Union** flag in municipal contexts, signaling a deliberate shift to make the party more acceptable to the mainstream right [[22]](https://www.gala.fr/l_actu/news_de_stars/du-rififi-entre-jordan-bardella-et-marine-le-pen-a-un-an-de-la-presidentielle-la-tension-monte-il-y-a-deux-camps-20260417). The party also secured a notable local victory, with **Louis Aliot** elected president of Perpignan's urban community, a success achieved through a deal with local right-wing parties, further demonstrating the **RN's** "dediabolisation" strategy [[18]](https://www.huffingtonpost.fr/politique/article/louis-aliot-profite-d-un-deal-avec-la-droite-pour-offrir-au-rassemblement-national-un-succes-majeur_262806.html). In Norway, the **Fremskrittspartiet** (**Frp**) is engaged in budget negotiations with **Høyre** [[8]](https://www.sandnesposten.no/onsker-ikke-at-kine-overgangen-skal-komme-mellom-frp-og-hoyre/s/5-105-1349202), while also experiencing a public misunderstanding regarding a joint seminar invitation with the far-left **Rødt** [[9]](https://www.aftenposten.no/norge/politikk/i/aJO922/marie-sneve-martinussen-sendte-ut-fellesinvitasjon-fra-roedt-og-frp-da-reagerte-frp-toppen). In Sweden, the **Sverigedemokraterna** are being criticized as having become a "normal right-wing party" after four years in power, accused of abandoning working-class voters in favor of the wealthy, and linked to a "troll factory" operation reminiscent of "Putin's Russia" [[10]](https://www.expressen.se/kultur/victor-malm/jimmie-akesson-har-redan-vunnit-kriget/) [[11]](https://www.dalademokraten.se/2026-04-19/lars-isacsson-s-socialdemokraterna-har-gjort-laxan/).

## Economic and Governance Ideologies

Economic concerns and critiques of established governance models were interwoven into various right-wing narratives. In the UK, the revelations about **Reform UK** deputy leader **Richard Tice's** alleged failure to pay nearly £100,000 in corporation tax through shell companies underscore a potential conflict between the party's anti-establishment rhetoric and the financial practices of its leaders, with a significant amount of money transferred to the party [[34]](https://nation.cymru/news/tice-says-errors-bound-to-happen-after-reports-he-failed-to-pay-100000-tax/) [[35]](https://taxpolicy.org.uk/2026/04/18/richard-tice-quidnet-reit-corporation-tax/) [[36]](https://www.thetimes.com/uk/politics/article/richard-tice-corporation-tax-reform-ttq9cp60f). The continued payment of £1.3 billion to the **EU** by the UK in 2025, years after Brexit, was framed as a "Brexit bombshell," raising questions about the Withdrawal Agreement's long-term liabilities and the UK's leverage in future negotiations [[83]](https://www.express.co.uk/news/politics/2195446/brexit-bombshell-shocking-report-reveals/amp) [[84]](https://www.express.co.uk/news/politics/2195446/brexit-bombshell-shocking-report-reveals). This narrative suggests that Brexit, despite being enacted, still imposes significant financial burdens.

Former UK Prime Minister **Liz Truss** positioned herself as an advocate for economic reform, attributing the UK's "decline" to high taxes, excessive regulation, and the "debasement of our currency." She expressed interest in **bitcoin** as a solution to centralized control and inflation, and announced the launch of **CPAC UK** to promote a "sovereignty and liberty" movement, aligning with a broader global conservative agenda [[80]](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/04/18/former-uk-prime-minister-sees-economy-on-very-negative-trajectory-indicates-support-for-bitcoin). This reflects a growing trend among some right-wing figures to embrace cryptocurrencies as a hedge against perceived governmental overreach and inflationary monetary policies. The **Lega** leader **Matteo Salvini** at the Milan rally explicitly attacked the "malefica accoppiata" (maleficent coupling) of the **European Union** and the **IMF**, and advocated for stopping the Green Deal and a return to buying gas and oil from Russia, highlighting a protectionist and anti-globalist economic stance [[28]](https://tg24.sky.it/politica/2026/04/18/manifestazione-lega-milano-salvini) [[30]](https://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/politica/2026/04/18/a-milano-corteo-dei-patrioti-e-della-lega-in-piazza-duomo.-salvini-chiude_c310da29-ad7e-4873-af8f-09317aa97b3f.html).
